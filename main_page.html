Introduction

The rubik's cube has been around for almost 50 years now and it has evolved immensely. First the 3x3 and then came the 2x2, 4x4, 5x5, 6x6, all the way to the 16x16. Not only do we have so many different types of cubes to play with, they have also become extremely smooth and efficient to use and very creative with the mechanics.

Then came a new concept called speedcubing. Like its name suggests, it litterally means solving a puzzle very quickly. A puzzle that is not necessarily a cube. You could be solving a pyramix and some would refer to it as speedcubing.

However, a better term would be speedsolving.

In my opionion, the world of speedsolving is wonderful and broad. It is so satisfying to scramble and then solve a puzzle again and again. It could also easily get addicted to it so watch out for that as well. So if you haven't tried it out then why wait? This guide is right here for you!

Getting Started

To get started, you must have a puzzle. In this guide, we will be focusing on the 3x3 cube. So if you don't already have a 3x3 cube lying around at home then go ahead and buy one. It could be available in a toy store nearby or even in a supermarket. If are completely unable to find a cube at your local area then speedcubeshop is a very reliable online retailer that sells all the authentic cubes that you could think of. Next, if you are just starting out and have little to no knowledge of solving a 3x3 rubik's cube, go ahead and click here to go to the page in which I explain how to solve a cube using the beginner's method(CFOP).
